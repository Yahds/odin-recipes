Blueberry Shortcake

Description

Blueberry Shortcake is a delightful dessert that combines the natural sweetness of blueberries with the delicate charm of shortcake. This dessert offers a wonderful balance of flavors and textures, making it a favorite for many.

Blueberry Shortcake is perfect for gatherings, picnics, or any occasion where you want to indulge in a fruity and comforting dessert that showcases the best of seasonal blueberries.

Ingredients

Shortcakes

  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter

Blueberry Topper

  • 3 cups frozen blueberries
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Whipped Cream (optionial)

  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar

Steps

Shortcakes

  1. Gather your ingredients. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
  2. In a small bowl whisk together cream, egg, vanilla, and lemon zest, if desired; set aside.
  3. Combine flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt in a large bowl.
  4. Cut in butter with a pastry blender until the mixture is the consistency of coarse crumbs.
  5. Gently stir in cream mixture, just until moistened.
  6. Roll on a lightly floured surface to 3/4-inch thick.
  7. Cut biscuits using a 2 1/2- to 3-inch round cutter or cut the dough as desired.
  8. Place biscuits on the prepared baking sheet. Bake until golden brown, 15 minutes.
  9. Cool biscuits for 5 minutes on the baking sheet. Cool completely on a wire rack.

Blueberry Topper

  1. Combine 2 cups blueberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a medium saucepan.
  2. Bring mixture to a simmer and cook until thickened on medium-low, about 15 minutes, stirring regularly.
  3. Stir in remaining 1 cup blueberries. Cool for about 15 minutes.

Whipped Cream (if using)

  1. Add heavy whipping cream and powdered sugar to a medium bowl.
  2. Beat until medium peaks form, 3 to 4 minutes.

Assembly

  1. Split shortcakes in half horizontally.
  2. Spoon half of the blueberry compote on the bottom halves of the shortcakes.
  3. Stack the shortcake tops on top. Top with remaining blueberry compote and whipped cream, if using.
